# Module 7.14 Transport Management

The Transport Management module manages school transport operations including vehicles, routes, stops, driver assignments, student transport assignments, and transport-related fee tracking. It supports daily operational planning, improves parent visibility into assigned transport details, and helps schools manage a common K-12 operational requirement in a structured and auditable way.

The module enables schools to:
- Maintain vehicle and driver records
- Define routes and stops
- Sequence stops per route
- Assign students to transport routes and stops
- Track active and historical transport assignments
- Monitor transport capacity and route usage
- Record transport-related payment obligations where finance integration is enabled

The module must integrate with student records, parent relationships, fees, and attendance-related operational reporting. It must not create independent student identity records outside the platform’s SIS and RBAC foundations. 

***

## 1. Module Objective

Schools frequently manage buses, routes, stops, and student pickup allocations through paper registers, spreadsheets, and informal coordinator workflows. Without a structured transport workflow:
- Route assignments become error-prone
- Vehicle capacity is hard to control
- Student pickup and drop tracking becomes unreliable
- Parent visibility is poor
- Transport fees are inconsistently linked to student assignments
- Historical route and assignment changes are difficult to audit

Schools need this module to:
- Centralize transport setup and assignments
- Reduce manual route administration
- Improve parent transparency into pickup and drop details
- Track route usage and capacity
- Connect transport operations with fee and student data
- Preserve assignment history for operational and dispute resolution purposes

***

## 2. Why Schools Need It

### 2.1 Transport Is a Common K-12 Requirement
Many schools operate buses or vans and need structured transport records to manage daily student movement.

### 2.2 Improve Student Safety and Operational Clarity
Schools need clear route, stop, vehicle, and driver assignments so transport operations are predictable and auditable.

### 2.3 Reduce Administrative Work
Transport coordinators and admins should not need to manually maintain route maps, stop lists, and student allocations across multiple spreadsheets.

### 2.4 Improve Parent Transparency
Parents need visibility into assigned route, stop, pickup point, and relevant transport changes for their linked children, using the same relationship-based access model as the rest of the platform. 

### 2.5 Connect Operations and Finance
Where transport is fee-bearing, student transport assignments should connect to finance workflows rather than existing as disconnected operational records.

### 2.6 Support Measurable Daily Operations
Principals and School Admins need route utilization, vehicle capacity, stop count, and transport assignment metrics to make daily operations measurable.

## 5. Business Rules

### 5.1 Business Rules - Tenant Isolation
- **BR-001** Every vehicle, route, stop, driver, student transport assignment, and transport payment record must belong to exactly one tenant.
- **BR-002** Transport records cannot be accessed across tenants.
- **BR-003** All transport APIs and queries must automatically enforce tenant scope.

### 5.2 Business Rules - Vehicle Rules
- **BR-004** Vehicles must be uniquely identifiable within the tenant.
- **BR-005** Vehicle capacity must be configurable.
- **BR-006** Inactive vehicles cannot receive new route allocations.
- **BR-007** Historical vehicle usage must remain visible for past assignments.

### 5.3 Business Rules - Route and Stop Rules
- **BR-008** Routes must belong to a single tenant and school context.
- **BR-009** Stops must be reusable across routes only if school policy allows. Default behavior should treat stop-route ordering as route-specific through route stop mapping.
- **BR-010** Route stops must preserve ordered sequence.
- **BR-011** Inactive routes cannot receive new student assignments.
- **BR-012** Inactive stops cannot receive new student assignments.
- **BR-013** Route changes must be auditable.

### 5.4 Business Rules - Driver Rules
- **BR-014** Drivers must be recorded before vehicle or route assignment where driver tracking is enabled.
- **BR-015** A driver cannot be assigned to overlapping active route contexts if school policy tracks exclusive route ownership.
- **BR-016** Driver records must support active and inactive status without deleting historical references.

### 5.5 Business Rules - Student Assignment Rules
- **BR-017** Student transport assignments must reference an active student record from SIS. 
- **BR-018** Only active enrolled students may receive active transport assignments unless school policy explicitly allows future assignments. 
- **BR-019** A student may have only one active transport assignment per assignment type by default.
- **BR-020** Assignment history must never be deleted.
- **BR-021** Assignment changes must preserve effective start and end dates.
- **BR-022** Student transport assignment cannot reference inactive route or stop records.
- **BR-023** Parent visibility must follow parent-student relationship rules. 

### 5.6 Business Rules - Capacity and Validation Rules
- **BR-024** Vehicle and route capacity limits must be enforced before assignment confirmation.
- **BR-025** Over-capacity assignment attempts must be blocked or require authorized override based on school policy.
- **BR-026** Route utilization counts must use active assignment records.
- **BR-027** Student removal from transport must update capacity availability immediately.

### 5.7 Business Rules - Transport Payment Rules
- **BR-028** Transport payments, where enabled, must be linked to active student transport assignments.
- **BR-029** Transport assignment changes must not retroactively alter settled finance records.
- **BR-030** Finance integration should create forward-looking billing eligibility changes rather than rewriting historical payment records.
- **BR-031** Transport payment visibility for parents must follow finance and parent portal policy.

### 5.8 Business Rules - Audit and Reporting Rules
- **BR-032** Every create, edit, activate, deactivate, assign, unassign, and override action must be auditable.
- **BR-033** Transport reports must support filtering by route, stop, vehicle, student, and status.
- **BR-034** Historical transport assignments must remain searchable for operational review and dispute handling.

## 11. Edge Cases

- **EC-001** Student is assigned to a route that has reached capacity. System blocks assignment or requires override.
- **EC-002** Route is deactivated while students still have active assignments. New assignments are blocked and existing active records require review workflow.
- **EC-003** Vehicle is changed after route assignments exist. Historical route-vehicle associations remain preserved.
- **EC-004** Student withdraws from school while transport assignment is active. Assignment must end according to student status policy. 
- **EC-005** Parent linked after transport assignment creation. Child transport details become visible through relationship-based access once link is active. 
- **EC-006** Stop is removed from route while students are assigned there. Removal is blocked until reassignment or assignment closure.
- **EC-007** Driver becomes inactive while assigned to active route. Route flagged for operational review.
- **EC-008** Transport payment exists for a prior period after assignment ends. Historical finance record remains unchanged.
- **EC-009** Student changes stop within same route. Previous assignment history remains preserved with effective dates.
- **EC-010** Same student is assigned twice to active transport for same assignment type. Duplicate active assignment blocked.

## 12. Risks

### 12.1 Risks - Operational Risk
Incorrect route and stop assignments create daily transport confusion.  
Mitigation: Strong validation, searchable assignment screen, history preservation.

### 12.2 Risks - Capacity Risk
Vehicles or routes are over-assigned.  
Mitigation: Capacity enforcement, warnings, controlled override logging.

### 12.3 Risks - Privacy Risk
Parents access unrelated student transport details.  
Mitigation: Relationship-based filtering, strict tenant isolation, audited portal access. 

### 12.4 Risks - Finance Integrity Risk
Transport charges become disconnected from actual active assignments.  
Mitigation: Effective-dated assignments, forward-only billing updates, finance integration controls.

### 12.5 Risks - Compliance and Audit Risk
Historical route assignment disputes cannot be resolved later.  
Mitigation: Immutable event history, assignment effective dates, audit logs.

### 12.6 Risks - Scalability Risk
Large schools with many routes and students experience slow allocation workflows.  
Mitigation: Indexed route and student queries, pagination, optimized assignment views.

# Module 7.15 Library

The Library module manages book cataloging, copy-level inventory, book issue and return workflows, and fine tracking. It is an expansion feature that supports basic school library circulation without introducing the full complexity of advanced library management platforms.

The module enables schools to:
- Maintain a catalog of books
- Track individual book copies
- Issue books to students
- Record returns
- Calculate and track fines
- Provide circulation history and basic overdue visibility

The module must integrate with student records, RBAC, and parent visibility rules where applicable. It must not introduce separate user identity or student relationship models outside the platform foundation. 

***

## 1. Module Objective

Schools need a simple but reliable way to manage library circulation. Without a library workflow:
- Book inventory becomes unreliable
- Schools cannot track who borrowed which copy
- Return dates are missed
- Overdue fines are manually tracked
- Loss and damage handling becomes inconsistent
- Reporting on library usage becomes difficult

Schools need this module to:
- Track books and copies accurately
- Support issue and return workflows
- Record overdue and fine status
- Improve accountability for borrowed items
- Reduce manual library administration
- Preserve circulation history

***

## 2. Why Schools Need It

### 2.1 Support Book Circulation
A library system is needed to track lending and returns in an organized way.

### 2.2 Reduce Manual Recordkeeping
Schools should not depend on paper ledgers or spreadsheets to track book movements.

### 2.3 Improve Accountability
Each issued copy should be traceable to a borrower and return state.

### 2.4 Track Overdues and Fines
Schools need a structured way to identify overdue books and apply fine rules where enabled.

### 2.5 Improve Student Service
Students need a faster issue and return workflow with clear due dates and history.

### 2.6 Expand Platform Value
Library is an expansion feature that increases product coverage for schools that want broader operational digitization.

***

## 5. Business Rules

### 5.1 Business Rules - Tenant Isolation
- **BR-001** Every book, book copy, issue, return, and fine record must belong to exactly one tenant.
- **BR-002** Library records cannot be accessed across tenants.
- **BR-003** All library APIs and queries must automatically enforce tenant scope.

### 5.2 Book and Copy Rules
- **BR-004** Books represent catalog records.
- **BR-005** Book copies represent physical lendable instances.
- **BR-006** Each book copy must be uniquely identifiable within the tenant.
- **BR-007** Copy status must support availability tracking.
- **BR-008** Inactive or lost copies cannot be issued.

### 5.3 Issue and Return Rules
- **BR-009** Issues must reference a valid borrower record from the platform.
- **BR-010** Only active students may receive new issues in student-focused MVP unless later expanded to staff borrowing. 
- **BR-011** A book copy can have only one active issue at a time.
- **BR-012** Issue records must include issue date and due date.
- **BR-013** Returns must reference an existing active issue.
- **BR-014** A returned copy becomes available unless marked damaged, lost, or under review.
- **BR-015** Historical issue and return records must never be deleted.

### 5.4 Fine Rules
- **BR-016** Fines are optional and policy-driven.
- **BR-017** Fine calculation must be based on configured overdue rules where enabled.
- **BR-018** Fines may be waived only by authorized staff.
- **BR-019** Fine changes must be auditable.
- **BR-020** Fine settlement must not retroactively change original issue and return dates.

### 5.5 Parent and Student Visibility Rules
- **BR-021** Students may view only their own issue history.
- **BR-022** Parents may view only linked child library records where enabled. 
- **BR-023** Draft or internal audit notes must not be visible to parents or students.

### 5.6 Audit and Reporting Rules
- **BR-024** Every create, issue, return, fine, waive, and status-change action must be auditable.
- **BR-025** Overdue reporting must use current date against active issue due date.
- **BR-026** Historical circulation metrics must remain searchable and reportable.

## 11. Edge Cases

- **EC-001** Same book copy is issued twice concurrently. Operation blocked.
- **EC-002** Student becomes inactive while holding issued book. Historical issue remains active until return or administrative resolution. 
- **EC-003** Parent linked after issue date. Linked child library history becomes visible according to portal policy. 
- **EC-004** Book copy is marked lost after issue. Issue status and fine workflow update without deleting history.
- **EC-005** Return recorded with damaged condition. Copy becomes unavailable until reviewed.
- **EC-006** Fine is waived after overdue state was generated. Original overdue and issue history remain unchanged.
- **EC-007** Book metadata edited after historical issues exist. Historical issue records continue to reference the same copy identity.
- **EC-008** Copy is archived while issue remains active. Archival blocked or deferred until issue closure.
- **EC-009** Student tries to view another student’s issue history. Access denied.
- **EC-010** Duplicate copy code entered. Save blocked.

***

## 12. Risks

### 12.1 Risks - Inventory Integrity Risk
Book copy availability becomes inaccurate.  
Mitigation: Copy-level tracking, issue-return validation, status history.

### 12.2 Risks - Operational Risk
Issues and returns are recorded incorrectly or late.  
Mitigation: Simple circulation screen, overdue reporting, searchable history.

### 12.3 Risks - Privacy Risk
Parents view unrelated child library records.  
Mitigation: Relationship-based filtering, tenant-scoped access, portal restrictions. 

### 12.4 Risks - Fine Integrity Risk
Fine amounts become inconsistent with actual overdue state.  
Mitigation: Configurable fine rules, audit logs, controlled waiver actions.

### 12.5 Risks - Scalability Risk
Large catalog search and circulation history become slow.  
Mitigation: Indexed copy codes, searchable catalog, pagination, optimized overdue queries.

### 12.6 Risks - Product Scope Risk
Library complexity expands beyond MVP into full library-suite needs.  
Mitigation: Keep MVP limited to catalog, copies, issues, returns, and fines.
